======================================================================
VISUALIZATION INTERPRETATION
======================================================================

PCA Visualization:
----------------------------------------
PCA captures the directions of maximum variance in the embedding space.
In our visualizations, we observe that:
- Academic-related words tend to cluster together, reflecting their
  frequent co-occurrence in curriculum and program descriptions.
- Research words form a distinct cluster, as they appear in research
  pages and project descriptions in the corpus.
- Department/organizational words may overlap with research and academic
  clusters, as these concepts are interconnected at IIT Jodhpur.

t-SNE Visualization:
----------------------------------------
t-SNE preserves local neighborhood structure, making it better at
revealing fine-grained clusters. We observe:
- Tighter, more distinct clusters compared to PCA.
- Words that are semantically close (e.g., 'course' and 'semester')
  appear closer in t-SNE space.

CBOW vs Skip-gram Differences:
----------------------------------------
- CBOW tends to produce embeddings where frequent words are well-
  represented, leading to tighter clusters for common academic terms.
- Skip-gram captures more nuanced semantic relationships for rare
  words, which may result in more dispersed but informative clusters.
- Skip-gram embeddings often show better separation between semantically
  distinct clusters due to its word-context pair training approach.
